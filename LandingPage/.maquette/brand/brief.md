# ChatFlow Landing Page Brand Brief

## Product Summary

ChatFlow is a scrappy builder hack: it follows the Codex OAuth flow, obtains a valid access token, and uses that token to talk directly to Codex's realtime inference API. The landing page should make the trick obvious, funny, and technically legible.

## Audience

- Developers, AI tinkerers, indie builders, and open-source enjoyers.
- Readers who respect reverse engineering, proof-of-work, and code that runs.
- People likely arriving from GitHub, Hacker News, X/Twitter, Reddit, or a Show HN-style post.

## Positioning

This should feel like a third-party builder discovered an OpenAI-shaped capability path and made it legible. The vibe is "look what the official stack can do when a determined outsider follows the breadcrumbs", not "official OpenAI product page".

## Tone

- Irreverent but not reckless.
- Technically confident and specific.
- Self-aware about the duct tape.
- Short, punchy, and anti-corporate.

## Copy Constraints

- Lead with the OAuth-to-token-to-realtime trick.
- Prefer "clone it", "read the code", "see the trick", and "star it" over formal CTAs.
- Avoid startup-deck language like "robust", "enterprise", "next-generation", or "seamless".
- Include a small CYA note that signals the hack is grey-area and likely has a shelf life.

## Visual Direction

- OpenAI-adjacent product calm: spacious, precise, editorial, and demo-led.
- Third-party hack energy: one visible seam, one knowing wink, no fake official polish.
- Mostly warm off-white, black ink, soft grey surfaces, with restrained Codex green and packet blue accents.
- Use terminal/code surfaces as the proof, but keep the page airy enough to feel like a serious AI product demo.
- The demo/code snippet should feel like the main character.

## Brand Adjacency Guardrails

- Do not use OpenAI logos, knot marks, official wordmarks, product lockups, or copied page layouts.
- Make the page clearly third-party with copy such as "not official", "built outside the building", or "third-party duct tape".
- Borrow broad qualities only: restraint, whitespace, research-style diagrams, crisp typography, and calm confidence.
- Keep the hack visible so it does not become sanitized corporate AI marketing.

## Accessibility Requirements

- High color contrast for code, buttons, and annotations.
- Keyboard-visible focus states.
- Responsive navigation with a usable mobile menu if navigation is present.
- No document-level horizontal scroll at common widths.
